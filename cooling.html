Comfort cooling

Cooling systems

Cooling design is not only about peak capacity. Distribution, humidity, noise, zoning, controls and installation quality determine whether a system feels comfortable in daily use.

Split systems

Split systems are common for individual rooms and small zones. Good results depend on indoor unit placement, refrigerant pipe routing, condensate drainage and realistic expectations about airflow.

Ducted systems

Ducted cooling can hide equipment and serve multiple rooms, but it requires careful air distribution, balancing, access planning and noise control.

VRF / VRV systems

VRF and VRV systems provide flexible zoning for larger homes and small commercial buildings. They need clear refrigerant design, control coordination and maintenance planning.

Fan coils and hydronic cooling

Hydronic cooling with fan coils or radiant elements introduces water-side control, condensation prevention and changeover coordination with heating.

Common installation and control mistakes

Common problems include poor condensate routing, uncomfortable drafts, mismatched zoning, inaccurate sensor readings and controls that do not reflect how occupants use the rooms.
