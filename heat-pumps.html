System design

Heat pumps

Heat pump performance depends on the complete system: building load, water temperatures, emitters, hydraulic separation, defrost behavior, controls and commissioning.

Heating with heat pumps

In heating mode, a heat pump works best when the distribution system can deliver comfort with stable and moderate water temperatures. Weather compensation, flow stability and room feedback should support each other instead of fighting for control.

Cooling with heat pumps

Cooling with a heat pump requires attention to dew point risk, suitable emitters, condensate management and clear changeover logic. A system designed only for heating may not automatically be suitable for cooling.

Hydraulics and buffer tanks

Buffer tanks can help with volume, flow separation and cycling control, but they also introduce temperature mixing and sensor placement questions. Their role should be defined before pipework is built.

Control logic and sensors

Good control logic uses the right measurements for the right decision. Water temperature, room temperature, outdoor temperature, flow status and operating mode all describe different parts of the system.

Common design mistakes

Typical problems include unclear mode changeover, poor sensor location, missing hydraulic intent, oversized equipment, incompatible emitters and commissioning settings that are left at generic defaults.
